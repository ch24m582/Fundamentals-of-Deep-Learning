Chapter 1: Introduction

Problem 1.1

Show that the sum and product rules of probability can be written as: \[ p(X) = \sum_Y p(X, Y) \] \[ p(X, Y) = p(Y|X)p(X) \]

Solution:

Sum Rule:

The sum rule states that the marginal probability can be obtained by summing the joint probability over all possible values of the other variable.

Starting from the definition of joint probability:

\[ p(X) = \sum_{Y} p(X, Y) \]

This makes intuitive sense: to find the probability of X, we sum over all the ways X can occur with different values of Y.

Product Rule:

The product rule relates joint probability to conditional probability:

\[ p(X, Y) = p(Y|X)p(X) \]

We can derive this from the definition of conditional probability:

\[ p(Y|X) = \frac{p(X, Y)}{p(X)} \]

Multiplying both sides by \(p(X)\) gives us the product rule.

Note: These two rules form the foundation of probability theory and are used extensively throughout the book. The product rule can also be written symmetrically as \(p(X,Y) = p(X|Y)p(Y)\).
